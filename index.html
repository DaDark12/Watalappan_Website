History of Watalappan

Watalappan, a cherished Sri Lankan dessert, traces its roots back to the Malay community who brought it to the island centuries ago. It blends Sri Lanka’s tropical flavors — coconut milk, jaggery, and cardamom — to create a silky custard often served during Eid, weddings, and special celebrations.

Over time, this dessert became a national favorite, celebrated for its depth of flavor and rich texture. It symbolizes unity — combining traditions, sweetness, and warmth into one dish.

Recipe

  • 1 cup grated jaggery (or brown sugar)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ tsp cardamom powder
  • Pinch of nutmeg or clove (optional)
  • Cashew nuts for garnish

Melt the jaggery in a pan with a few tablespoons of water. Whisk eggs separately, then slowly add coconut milk and melted jaggery. Flavor with cardamom and nutmeg. Pour the mixture into a baking dish and steam or bake until firm. Garnish with roasted cashews and chill before serving.
