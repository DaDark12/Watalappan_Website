Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jaggery (grated and lightly melted)
  • 1 cup thick coconut milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 2-3 pandan leaves (optional)
  • Pinch of salt

Method:

  1. Grate jaggery and gently melt in a little water to form smooth syrup.
  2. Beat eggs lightly, add coconut milk, cardamom, nutmeg, and salt. Mix until smooth.
  3. Slowly whisk jaggery syrup into the coconut-egg mixture to avoid curdling.
  4. Strain mixture to remove lumps; add pandan leaves if using.
  5. Pour into a lightly greased baking dish. Cover with foil and cook in a water bath at 160Â°C (320Â°F) for 45â€“60 mins until set.

History & Culture

Watalappan originates from Sri Lanka with Malay culinary influence. Traditionally prepared during festivals, weddings, and religious occasions, it symbolizes celebration, family, and heritage.

The dessert highlights the tropical abundance of coconut and jaggery. Over generations, families perfected the custard to balance sweetness, creaminess, and aromatic spices. Regional variations may include extra cardamom, nutmeg, or pandan leaves.

Watalappan is not just a treat; it is a cultural ritual. Many households reserve it for special guests or festive gatherings, emphasizing its importance beyond mere taste. Its preparation and enjoyment remain a cherished family tradition across Sri Lanka.
