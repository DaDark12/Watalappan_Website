Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup jaggery (grated)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Pandan leaves (optional, 2-3 leaves)
  • Pinch of salt

Method:

  1. Grate jaggery and dissolve in a small amount of warm water to make syrup.
  2. Mix eggs lightly in a bowl. Add coconut milk, cardamom, nutmeg, and salt. Stir until smooth.
  3. Slowly add the jaggery syrup to the mixture, whisking gently to combine.
  4. Strain mixture to remove lumps and set aside pandan leaves if using.
  5. Pour mixture into a lightly greased baking dish. Cover with foil.
  6. Steam or bake in a water bath at 160Â°C (320Â°F) for 45â€“60 minutes until set.
  7. Remove and cool. Chill in refrigerator for a few hours before serving.
  8. Optional: Drizzle coconut cream or garnish with cashews before serving.

History & Culture

Watalappan is a traditional Sri Lankan dessert, influenced by Malay cuisine during the colonial period. Made primarily with coconut milk and jaggery, it became a staple at festive events such as weddings, Ramadan celebrations, and family gatherings.

The silky custard reflects Sri Lankaâ€™s love for coconut-based sweets, with each region sometimes adding subtle variations like cardamom, nutmeg, or pandan for fragrance. It is considered a royal dessert due to its rich taste and smooth texture.

Even today, Watalappan remains a cultural icon, representing heritage, family, and celebration. Its preparation is often passed down through generations, making it both a culinary and emotional tradition.
